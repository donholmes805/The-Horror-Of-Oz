Volume I: The Unmaking of Emerald

Chapter 1: Awakening in Ash

flare

The air tasted of burnt copper and ancient ozone. When my eyes finally opened, the sky was not the brilliant, sapphire dome I remembered from the chronicles of the Great Oz. It was a bruised purple, choked with the heavy, falling soot of a world being unmade. I lay upon the cold, uneven surface of what used to be the Yellow Brick Road, but the path was no longer gold. It was a scorched, sickly yellow, like the teeth of a dying giant.

I reached out, my fingers brushing against the rough masonry. The stones felt brittle, as if the very magic that held them together had evaporated into the fog. Somewhere in the distance, a sound echoed—not the cheerful song of a Munchkin choir, but a low, rhythmic grinding of heavy iron gears. The Great Clock of Time was stuttering, and with it, the reality of this land.

"The heart that was once given is now a hollow cage, and the brain that once thought is lost to the grey mist."

A lantern flickered nearby, its flame a cold, bioluminescent green. It hung from a gnarled, leafless tree that looked like a hand clawing its way out of the earth. I stood up, my joints popping like dry twigs. My shadow stretched long and distorted against the cracked path, dancing to the tune of the flickering light. I was alone, yet I felt the weight of a thousand unseen eyes watching from the shifting shadows of the Poppy Fields—now a graveyard of withered stalks.

I checked my pack. The shards were still there, pulsing with a faint, rhythmic heat. Twelve hundred shards—the cost of a soul in this decaying paradise. If the legends were true, the Wizard hadn't left; he had simply retreated into the machinery of the city, becoming the very ghost in the shell that now haunted our dreams. I took a step forward, the ash crunching beneath my boots, and began my long walk toward the silhouette of the jagged spires on the horizon.
